In payments, data governance is an operational capability, not a document.

## 1. Classification

Define clear categories:

- sensitive data (PII)
- financial data
- operational metadata

Each category needs distinct access and retention rules.

## 2. Traceability

Every relevant change must answer:

- who accessed
- what was modified
- when it happened

Without traceability, there is no effective audit.

## 3. Quality

Minimum metrics:

- completeness
- uniqueness
- consistency across sources

## 4. Retention and Minimisation

Retain only what is strictly necessary for operations, compliance, and support.

## 5. Context-Based Access

Implement controls by role and by use case. Avoid broad permanent privileges.

## Closing

Governing payment data means reducing systemic risk without blocking the business.
