Effective observability does not start with dashboards. It starts by defining what "healthy service" means for the user.

## 1. User-Oriented SLIs

Useful examples:

- public API availability
- p95 latency of critical operations
- payment success rate

Avoid internal metrics as the sole health signal.

## 2. Realistic SLOs

An aggressive SLO without operational capacity generates fatigue. Define:

- target (e.g. 99.9%)
- measurement window (28–30 days)
- allowed error budget

## 3. Alerts That Drive Action

An alert must answer: who acts and what action they take.

- page alert: active user impact
- ticket alert: degradation without immediate impact
- no runbook, no valid alert

## 4. Burn Rate

Use burn rate alerts to detect issues quickly and avoid over-alerting.

## Closing

The goal is not to observe more, but to make better decisions with less noise.
